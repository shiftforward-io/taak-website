CFO ( Chief Financial Officer )

  • Full time employee
  • Revenue between $ 10 Million to $ 50 Million revenue
  • Role involves entire Finance & Accounting Domain
  • Strategy role limited and often CEO is involved in strategy
  • Securing financial backing

Fractional CFO

  • Part time with limited hours per week
  • Revenue less than $ 10 Million
  • Provides financial insights, advise on strategy & optimization of internal processes
  • Provides advise on scaling strategy
  • Provides Connections & Insights on how to raise

A CFO, or Chief Financial Officer, assumes the pinnacle financial role within an organization, bearing the responsibility for the economic well-being of the enterprise. Their duties span a spectrum, including but not confined to assembling an exceptional finance and accounting team, maintaining equilibrium between revenues and expenses.

Overseeing FP&A functions, providing counsel on mergers and acquisitions, securing financial backing, collaborating with department heads to scrutinize financial data and devise budgets, affirming the accuracy of reports, and advising boards of directors and the CEO on strategic initiatives.
