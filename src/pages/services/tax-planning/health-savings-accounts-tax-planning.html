A tax-advantaged way to save for medical expenses while enjoying potential tax benefits.

THE EASIEST WAY

An HSA is a savings account that allows individuals covered by a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) to set aside money on a pre-tax basis to pay for qualified medical expenses.

Tax Deductions

  • Contributions to an HSA are tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income for the year.
  • Contributions made by your employer are excluded from your gross income.

Tax-Free Growth

  • Any interest or other earnings on the assets in the HSA are tax-free.
  • The account balance can grow and be withdrawn tax-free when used for qualified medical expenses.

Portable and Flexible

  • HSAs are portable, meaning you can keep the account even if you change employers, health plans, or retire.
  • There's no "use it or lose it" rule - unused funds roll over from year to year.

Qualified Medical Expenses

  • Funds from an HSA can be used to pay for a wide range of qualified medical expenses, including deductibles, copayments, and certain non-prescription medications.
  • Leverage appreciated securities for charitable giving to potentially enhance tax benefits.

Retirement Savings

  • After age 65, you can withdraw funds from your HSA for non-medical expenses without penalty. However, regular income tax applies to the withdrawal.
