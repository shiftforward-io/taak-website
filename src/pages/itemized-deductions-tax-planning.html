Understand Eligible Expenses

Our experienced advisors work closely with you to create customized retirement strategies tailored to your unique financial situation and goals. We adhere strictly to IRS guidelines, providing you with a roadmap to a financially secure retirement.

Medical Expenses

Deductible medical expenses include health insurance premiums, qualified medical treatments, prescription medications, and certain medical-related travel costs.

Keep detailed records, including receipts and statements, to substantiate your medical expense deductions.

Homeownership Deductions

Deduct mortgage interest and property taxes paid on your primary residence and, in some cases, a second home.

Points paid on a mortgage for the purchase or improvement of a primary residence may also be deductible.

Charitable Contributions

Donate to qualified charitable organizations to claim deductions. Keep records of cash, property, or goods donated.

Volunteer expenses, such as mileage, may be deductible. Maintain accurate logs and documentation.

State and Local Taxes (SALT)

Deduct state income taxes, property taxes, and other local taxes paid during the tax year.

Be aware of any IRS limitations on the total deduction for state and local taxes.

Job-Related Expenses

Deduct unreimbursed job-related expenses, such as work-related travel, education, and professional dues.

Maintain detailed records, including receipts and documentation of the business purpose.

Miscellaneous Deductions

Certain miscellaneous expenses, such as unreimbursed casualty losses, tax preparation fees, and certain investment expenses, may be deductible.

Be aware of the IRS limitations and restrictions on miscellaneous deductions.
